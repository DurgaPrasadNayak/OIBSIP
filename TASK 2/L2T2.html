RATAN TATA

The untold story of India's finest Business Tycoon

Ratan Tata says "I don't believe in taking right decisions...I take the desicions and make them right.."

Here's a time line of Ratan Tata's life:

  • 1937 - Born in Mumbai, India
  • 1948 - His parents separated, and he was subsequently raised and adopted by Navajbai Tata, his grandmother and widow of Ratanji Tata.
  • 1955 - Completed his schooling from Riverdale country school in New York
  • 1959 - Graduated with a Bacherlor's degree in Architecture from Cornell University
  • 1970 - Tata was given a managerial position in the Tata group. He achieved initial success by turning the subsidiary National Radio and Electronics (NELCO) around, only to see it collapse during an economic slowdown
  • 1991 - stepped down as chairman of Tata Sons, naming him his successor.
  • 2010 - In 2010, Tata Group companies and Tata charities donated $50 million for the construction of an executive center at Harvard Business School (HBS). The executive center has been named Tata Hall, after Ratan Tata (AMP '75), chairman emeritus of Tata Sons.
  • 2012 - Upon turning 75, Ratan Tata resigned his executive powers in the Tata group on 28 December 2012.
  • 2013 - he was appointed to the board of trustees of the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace.
  • 2015 - In April 2015, it was reported that Tata had acquired a stake in Chinese smartphone startup Xiaomi.
  • 2015 - Ratan took an advisory role at Kalari Capital, a venture capital firm founded by Vani Kola.
  • 2016 - he invested in Teabox, an online premium Indian Tea seller,[29] and CashKaro.com, a discount coupons and cash-back website.
  • 2018 - Tata Hall at the University of California, San Diego (UC San Diego), opened in November 2018, houses facilities for the biological and physical sciences and is the home of the Tata Institute for Genetics and Society.

Awards Received..

  • 2000 - Received Padma Bhushan.
  • 2008 - Received Padma Vibhushan.
  • 2006 - Received 'Maharashtra Bhushan' in 2006 for his huge work in the public administration in Maharashtra
  • 2021 - Received 'Assam Baibhav' in 2021 for his exceptional contribution towards furthering cancer care in Assam.
